A little about Post-Rock


Post-Rock is an experimental style of rock and roll that emphasizes crescendos. Post-rock uses instruments characteristic of traditional rock music, however,it utilizes rhythms to create timbre and textures not found in traditional rock. The style could be classified as "atmospheric", however, different artists tend towards more atmospheric soundscapes than others.


Due to the slow build up of the music within the post-rock genre, it can be a difficult to appreciate style. However, with some patience, one can enjoy some of the most beautiful, almost classical-esque rock available today.


Post-rock seemed to appear on the scene during the 1990s although it is apparent that the style had been building slowly through experimental music starting in the 1960s. Bands such as the Velvet Underground (60's), David Bowie (70's), and Swans(80's) all showcase elements of Post-Rock. A band called "Slint" with their release "Spiderland" in 1991 marks the full blossom of the Post-Rock sound. Later, Godspeed You! Black Emperor truly refined the sound and are the most influential Post-Rock band to this day.
